Frequently Asked Questions

Are rebounders safe for people with joint problems?

Yes, rebounders are specifically designed for low-impact cardio. The bouncing motion is gentler on joints than running or high-impact exercise. However, consult your doctor if you have serious joint conditions. Boogie Bounce equipment is engineered to minimize impact while maximizing workout effectiveness.

How often should I use a rebounder for best results?

For optimal cardiovascular benefits, aim for 20-30 minute sessions 3-5 times per week. Boogie Bounce rebounders support various workout intensities, allowing you to adjust frequency and duration based on your fitness level and goals.

What's the difference between a rebounder and a mini trampoline?

While the terms are often used interchangeably, rebounders are specifically designed for fitness workouts with professional-grade springs and stability, while mini trampolines may be more recreational. Boogie Bounce specializes in fitness-focused rebounders built for serious cardio training.

Can rebounders help with weight loss?

Yes, rebounder workouts can burn significant calories while improving cardiovascular health. A 30-minute session can burn 200-400 calories depending on intensity. Combined with proper nutrition, Boogie Bounce rebounders support effective weight management programs.

Do I need any special shoes or flooring for a rebounder?

Supportive athletic shoes are recommended, though many users bounce barefoot. Place your rebounder on a level, stable surface. Boogie Bounce rebounders work well on most home flooring, but a yoga mat underneath provides extra protection for your floors.
