Frequently Asked Questions

Can the Matador handle weighted pull-ups with a belt?

Absolutely. I've used it with a dip belt holding 45 pounds regularly. The bar itself has zero flex, and the base stays completely locked. The weight distribution through the wider footprint is why it works—this isn't a doorway hack. Just make sure you have enough clearance above the bar so the belt doesn't swing into the frame.

Is assembly really necessary, or can I set it up without tools?

You'll need basic tools—an adjustable wrench and Allen keys come with most kits, but confirm yours does. The bolts require genuine tightening, not finger-tight. Loose hardware turns your expensive bar into a liability. Budget 30-45 minutes your first time. If you're not mechanically comfortable, this is worth paying someone $50 to assemble correctly.

How does this compare to a doorway pull-up bar for a small apartment?

Total different category. Doorway bars are space-savers but limit weight safely, feel unstable with heavy loads, and can damage door frames over time. The Matador takes floor space but gives you a professional-grade experience. If you're in a small apartment, stick with a doorway bar unless you have a dedicated corner. If you have even modest garage or basement space, the Matador's stability is worth the footprint.

Will this rust if I keep it in an unheated garage?

Mine has survived three months in an unheated space with temperature swings, and zero rust yet. Rogue's finish is solid. That said, a light coat of WD-40 on the bar every few months in humid climates (or after sweaty summer sessions) adds insurance. Check bolts periodically for looseness, especially in your first month as everything settles.
