Frequently Asked Questions

How do the SelectTech 1090s compare to cheaper adjustable dumbbell brands?

Cheaper brands ($200-300 for a pair) often use identical mechanisms with different branding. The real differences are build durability and dial responsiveness over 5+ years of use. If you plan to own these for a decade, Bowflex's reputation for not developing loose plates justifies the upcharge. If you're unsure about long-term commitment, a budget brand is a smarter first purchase.

Can I use these for serious strength training, or are they only for casual fitness?

Serious strength training is absolutely possible up to the 90 lb maximum. Where they fall short is for progressive overload at very high weights—powerlifters or advanced lifters who need 100+ lb dumbbells will outgrow them. For hypertrophy training, general strength, and conditioning work, the 5-90 lb range covers 95% of home lifters. The dial mechanism is reliable enough for regular heavy use.

Is June really a good time to buy these compared to other months?

June sits at the tail end of New Year's Resolution purchasing (when deals are picked over) but before July 4th sales kick in. If you can wait until mid-July, retailers often run Independence Day promotions. That said, prices on this product stay relatively stable year-round. Buy when you're ready to commit, not based on expecting dramatic seasonal discounts.

How much space do these actually take up when stored?

In collapsed form, a pair occupies roughly the footprint of a shoebox stacked vertically—approximately 3-4 inches thick and 10 inches long. This is genuinely space-saving compared to a full dumbbell rack, but understand they're still heavier and more awkward to store than resistance bands or yoga mats. A closet shelf or under-bed storage works, but they're not invisible.

Do the dial adjustments ever get stuck or unreliable over time?

Across hundreds of verified reviews, dial sticking is rare but not nonexistent—typically appearing after 2+ years with heavier users. Most reported issues resolve with cleaning around the dial mechanism. Bowflex's warranty covers mechanical failures, which provides real peace of mind at this price point. This reliability track record is part of why they cost more than unknown brands.
