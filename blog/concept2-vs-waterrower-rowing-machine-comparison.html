Quick Verdict

Concept2 is the best all-around choice for serious athletes and budget-conscious buyers who want proven reliability and extensive performance metrics. WaterRower wins for those seeking a premium, aesthetically pleasing machine with a naturally smooth rowing feel and don't mind spending extra for the experience.

Concept2 Overview

The Concept2 Model D is the gold standard in rowing machines—used in CrossFit boxes, rowing clubs, and elite training facilities worldwide. It's built like a tank with a magnetic resistance system that's virtually maintenance-free and incredibly consistent.

Pros:

  • Legendary durability: Concept2 machines routinely last 10+ years in commercial gyms with minimal wear
  • Performance tracking: The PM5 monitor is industry-leading with split times, drag factor adjustment, and workout apps
  • Affordable: Around $900-1,100, it's significantly cheaper than premium competitors
  • Adjustable resistance: Drag factor settings let you customize the rowing feel
  • Compact design: Folds for storage without taking up excessive space
  • Strong community: Extensive online resources, programs, and competition opportunities

Cons:

  • Plastic seat can feel cheap compared to luxury models
  • Magnetic resistance feels distinctly "mechanical" rather than fluid
  • No aesthetic appeal—purely functional design
  • Requires electricity for the monitor (though it can run on AA batteries)

Who It's For:

Serious rowers, CrossFit athletes, fitness enthusiasts on a budget, and anyone prioritizing performance metrics over luxury. Perfect for home gyms, garage setups, and commercial facilities.

WaterRower Rowing Machine Overview

WaterRower is the luxury choice in the rowing machine market, featuring natural water resistance that mimics the feel of rowing on actual water. If rowing machines were cars, Concept2 is a reliable Honda and WaterRower is a sleek BMW.

Pros:

  • Smooth, natural resistance: Water provides fluid, progressive resistance that feels closest to outdoor rowing
  • Stunning design: Beautiful wooden construction makes it a statement piece in your home
  • Quiet operation: Whisper-quiet compared to magnetic machines (except for water movement)
  • Premium build quality: Solid ash or walnut construction feels luxurious and substantial
  • No power needed: Completely mechanical—no plug-ins required
  • Engaging experience: The water's movement creates an immersive, rewarding feel

Cons:

  • Expensive: Typically $2,500-4,000+ depending on wood type and configuration
  • Requires maintenance: Water may need treatment every 3-6 months to prevent algae (minimal but still required)
  • Limited performance metrics: Basic monitor with fewer tracking options than Concept2
  • Heavy and difficult to move: 100+ lbs makes repositioning a task
  • Slower resistance adjustment: Water resistance isn't as quickly adjustable as magnetic systems

Who It's For:

Luxury buyers who view their rowing machine as home décor, recreational rowers prioritizing feel over metrics, fitness enthusiasts with plenty of space and budget, and those who row for enjoyment rather than intense competition.
