Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need to mount this into wall studs?

Absolutely. Wall studs are non-negotiable for safety. Mounting into drywall alone will eventually fail under loaded squats or bench presses. Use a stud finder, locate your 16-inch on-center studs, and anchor accordingly. This isn't a shortcut situation.

How much space does this actually save compared to a traditional power rack?

A standard power rack occupies roughly 8-10 square feet of permanent floor space. When folded flat, the wall-mounted version uses that same footprint vertically against your wall, freeing up 6-8 square feet of usable floor space. That's the difference between a cluttered garage and actual training area.

Can I do bench press safely on this, or is it squat-only?

You can definitely bench press on a quality wall-mounted rack like Titan's model, but you'll need adjustable safeties set at the correct height for your bench. Some users prefer dedicated bench attachments. The versatility is there—your setup matters.

What's the installation difficulty level for someone with basic handyman skills?

If you can use a drill and own a stud finder, installation is manageable in 1-2 hours. The challenge isn't technical—it's finding studs and ensuring level mounting. Take your time with the initial setup, and you'll avoid headaches later.
