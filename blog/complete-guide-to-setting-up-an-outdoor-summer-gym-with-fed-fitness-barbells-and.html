Planning Your Outdoor Gym Space

Before purchasing equipment, assess your available space and climate conditions. Your outdoor gym needs a level surface—concrete, asphalt, or treated ground—with at least 8x10 feet for basic setup and up to 12x15 feet for a full training area. Consider weather exposure: if your location experiences heavy rain or intense UV rays, invest in protective coverings for your FED Fitness equipment. Measure your space carefully and create a layout that allows safe movement between stations. Most lifters benefit from positioning a power rack or squat stand in the center, with plate storage nearby and cardio machines on the periphery.

Essential FED Fitness Equipment for Your Outdoor Setup

FED Fitness specializes in strength equipment that's built to perform outdoors. Start with a quality Olympic barbell—FED Fitness barbells feature knurled grips and durable sleeves rated for heavy loads. Next, invest in weight plates that match your training goals; FED Fitness offers standard and competition-grade plates in various weights, all constructed to resist rust and weathering. A power rack or squat stand is essential for safety during compound movements like squats and bench presses. FED Fitness power racks provide stable, adjustable platforms that can accommodate various exercises. Complete your setup with dumbbells for accessory work and a weight bench for pressing movements. Adding Cardio and Functional Training Equipment

While strength training is the foundation, incorporating cardio and functional equipment maximizes your outdoor gym's versatility. FED Fitness cardio machines, such as rowing machines and assault bikes, provide excellent conditioning options without requiring gym membership. Power towers are another valuable addition, allowing bodyweight exercises like pull-ups, dips, and leg raises. For functional training, consider a FED Fitness dumbbell set—they're weather-resistant and perfect for movements that improve explosive power and conditioning. These additions complement your barbell training and prevent workout monotony throughout the summer months.

Protection and Maintenance for Longevity

Outdoor equipment requires regular maintenance to prevent rust and degradation. FED Fitness equipment is constructed from high-quality materials, but protection extends its lifespan significantly. Invest in weatherproof covers, rubber matting, and a small storage shed or canopy to shield your equipment from excessive rain and UV exposure. After each use, wipe down barbells and plates, especially after sweating on them. Apply a light coat of silicone spray monthly to prevent rust on metal components. Check FED Fitness equipment periodically for loose bolts or wear, tightening as needed. Proper maintenance ensures your outdoor gym remains safe, functional, and visually appealing throughout summer and beyond.
