Step 1: Assess Your Space and Budget

Before purchasing any equipment, take time to evaluate your available space and establish a realistic budget. Measure the area where you plan to set up your home gym—whether it's a garage, basement, spare room, or backyard. Consider ceiling height for exercises like overhead presses, and ensure you have room for movement around your equipment.

Next, determine how much you're willing to spend. A complete home gym can range from $500 to $5,000+, depending on your needs. Start by prioritizing essentials: a quality weight bench and barbell should be your foundation. FED Fitness offers equipment at various price points, making it easier to build a gym that fits your budget without compromising on quality.

Step 2: Choose Your Weight Bench

A weight bench is the cornerstone of any home gym. Look for benches that offer adjustability (flat, incline, decline positions) as this expands your exercise options significantly. Stability is crucial—ensure the bench can handle your maximum weight plus the barbell weight with a safety margin.

FED Fitness provides a range of weight benches designed for durability and comfort. Their benches feature reinforced frames, comfortable padding, and smooth adjustment mechanisms. Whether you need a simple flat bench for beginners or an advanced multi-position bench for experienced lifters, FED Fitness has options that deliver exceptional value and performance.

Step 3: Select Your Barbell and Plates

The barbell is your primary lifting tool. For home use, a standard Olympic barbell (45 lbs) is ideal for most people. Pair it with weight plates that match your strength level and goals. Start with a variety of plate denominations to allow for progressive overload training.

FED Fitness offers premium Olympic barbells with excellent grip, durability, and balance. Their selection of weight plates includes standard and bumper plates, so you can choose based on your flooring and training style. High-quality plates from FED Fitness ensure smooth, consistent lifts and long-term reliability.

Step 4: Add Additional Strength Equipment

Once you have your basic setup, expand your gym with complementary equipment. A power rack provides safety for heavy lifts and allows you to perform squats, bench presses, and rows with confidence. Dumbbells offer versatility for isolation exercises and conditioning work. Power towers enable bodyweight exercises like pull-ups and dips.

FED Fitness supplies all these additions and more. Their power racks are engineered for stability and safety, while their dumbbell collections provide options for every strength level. By building with FED Fitness equipment, you create a cohesive, reliable gym that supports every aspect of your strength training journey.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the minimum amount of space needed for a home gym?

A basic home gym requires approximately 100-150 square feet. This provides room for a weight bench, barbell, and plates, plus space to move safely during exercises. If adding a power rack, allocate additional vertical and horizontal space.

How much weight should I buy for my home gym?

Start with 150-200 lbs of total weight if you're beginning. This allows for progressive training while remaining affordable. As you progress, add more plates incrementally. Most experienced lifters benefit from 300-500+ lbs depending on their strength level.

Is FED Fitness equipment suitable for beginners?

Absolutely. FED Fitness offers equipment across all skill levels. Their weight benches, barbells, and plates are designed with safety and ease of use in mind, making them perfect for beginners while maintaining the durability and performance experienced lifters demand.

What's the best order to purchase home gym equipment?

Prioritize in this order: weight bench, barbell, weight plates, dumbbells, power rack, and cardio machines. This progression builds a functional gym while allowing you to spread costs over time.

Do I need a power rack for a home gym?

While not essential, a power rack significantly enhances safety for heavy lifts like squats and bench presses. It's highly recommended if you plan to lift heavy or train alone. FED Fitness power racks offer excellent safety features at competitive prices.
