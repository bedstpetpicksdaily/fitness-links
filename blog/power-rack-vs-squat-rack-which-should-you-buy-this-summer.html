What's the Difference Between a Power Rack and Squat Rack?

While the terms are sometimes used interchangeably, power racks and squat racks have important structural differences. A squat rack typically features two vertical posts with adjustable safety bars and a barbell holder—it's designed specifically for squats and standing exercises. A power rack, on the other hand, is a fully enclosed cage with four vertical posts and multiple attachment points, allowing you to perform a wider variety of exercises safely.

FED Fitness manufactures both types with commercial-grade construction, ensuring stability and durability for heavy lifting. Their power racks feature robust steel frames and precision-engineered safety pins that give you confidence when pushing your limits.

Power Racks: Versatility and Safety

Power racks are the all-in-one solution for serious lifters. The enclosed cage design provides maximum safety, especially when training alone. You can perform squats, bench presses, overhead presses, rows, and countless other movements within the same equipment. The adjustable safety bars catch the barbell if you fail a rep, making it ideal for progressive overload training.

FED Fitness power racks come with multiple attachment options for pull-up bars, dip stations, and cable machines, transforming your entire training approach. The versatility means you won't outgrow this equipment as your strength increases. For serious home gym builders, FED Fitness power racks are engineered to handle competition-level training.

Squat Racks: Space-Efficient and Focused

If space is limited or you primarily focus on squats and overhead presses, a squat rack offers a compact alternative. It requires significantly less floor space than a power rack while still providing essential safety features. The open design allows for better airflow and visibility, making it feel less claustrophobic during intense sessions.

FED Fitness squat racks deliver professional-grade performance in a smaller footprint. Their models feature adjustable height settings and sturdy construction that handles heavy loads, perfect for athletes who want specialized equipment without the bulk. Combined with other FED Fitness equipment like weight benches and barbells, you can still build a complete training station.

Choosing Based on Your Needs

Consider your training goals, available space, and budget when deciding. If you perform diverse exercises and want maximum safety features, a power rack is the superior investment. If you're focused on lower body development and have limited space, a squat rack delivers excellent results at a lower price point. Both FED Fitness options represent outstanding value in strength equipment, backed by reliable construction and warranty support.

Can you use a squat rack for bench press?

Some squat racks have adjustable benches, but most aren't designed for safe barbell bench pressing. Power racks from FED Fitness are ideal for bench press with proper safety pin placement.

Squat racks typically need 4x4 feet minimum. Power racks require 8x8 feet or more for full functionality and exercise variety. FED Fitness offers multiple size options.
