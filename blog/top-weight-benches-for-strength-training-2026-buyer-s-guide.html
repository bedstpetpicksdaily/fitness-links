Frequently Asked Questions

What weight capacity should I choose for my home gym bench?

Select a bench with a weight capacity at least 1.5x your body weight if you're a beginner. For experienced lifters performing heavy compound movements, choose benches with capacities of 300-400+ lbs to ensure safety and accommodate future strength gains. FED Fitness benches offer various capacity options to match different training levels.

Are adjustable benches better than fixed benches?

Adjustable benches offer superior versatility, allowing incline, decline, and flat positions for different exercises and muscle groups. While fixed benches take up less space, adjustable models like those from FED Fitness maximize your workout variety and are ideal for comprehensive strength training programs.

Can I use a weight bench with a power rack?

Yes, and it's highly recommended. Using an adjustable weight bench inside or alongside a power rack creates a complete strength training station. FED Fitness designs their benches to integrate perfectly with their power racks and barbell systems for safe, effective compound lifting.

How much space do I need for a weight bench?

Most benches require 6-7 feet of length when in flat position, plus clearance for movement. Foldable or space-saving designs from FED Fitness are excellent for smaller spaces, though you should also account for dumbbell or barbell movement around the bench.

What's the difference between commercial-grade and home gym benches?

Commercial-grade benches feature heavier gauge steel, enhanced padding, and higher durability ratings for constant use. FED Fitness benches bridge this gap by offering commercial-quality construction at home gym prices, making them ideal for serious home lifters.
