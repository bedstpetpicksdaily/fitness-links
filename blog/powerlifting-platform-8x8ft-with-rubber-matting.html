Frequently Asked Questions

Will an 8x8 platform fit in a standard garage with one car parked?

Barely, but awkwardly. A two-car garage gives you comfortable side-by-side parking with the platform. If you have only one spot, the platform takes most of it. Measure your actual floor space first—this isn't a squeeze-it-in situation.

Do I need to bolt it down or secure it somehow?

No bolting required for the rubber matting itself, but the platform should sit on a clean, level floor. Some people add shims under high spots. Movement is minimal once weights are on it, so anchoring is usually unnecessary unless you're in a second-story space.

How long does rubber matting actually last with heavy training?

Quality rubber (at least 3/8-inch thickness) holds up for 4-6 years of consistent training—that's 3-5 sessions weekly. Cheaper, thinner versions show compression and wear within 18-24 months. If you're serious about lifting, spend the extra $150-$200 for thicker material upfront.

Can I use this for exercises other than deadlifts?

Absolutely. Cleans, snatches, heavy squats from the floor, and even kettlebell work all benefit. The platform provides the same stability and noise reduction. It's really a general heavy-lifting platform, not just a deadlift setup.
