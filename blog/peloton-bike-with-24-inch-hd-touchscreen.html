Peloton Bike+ Review: Worth the Investment in 2026?

The Peloton Bike+ has been sitting in my home gym for the better part of six months now, and I've logged enough miles on it to have genuine opinions—not just marketing talking points. This isn't a casual spin bike; it's a connected fitness machine that demands real estate, real money, and real commitment. The 24-inch HD touchscreen is legitimately impressive when you're mid-class, and the bike's engineering feels solid underneath you. But before you drop thousands of dollars, you need to know exactly what you're getting and, more importantly, what you're missing.

Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ The 24-inch HD touchscreen is genuinely immersive during classes—I've tested cheaper alternatives, and the difference is noticeable. Instructors feel closer, metrics are crisp, and the screen responsiveness means no lag when adjusting resistance or cadence mid-ride.
  • ✓ The rotating screen design solves a real problem. I can flip it to face different directions for strength classes or streaming content without dismounting, which sounds minor until you're actually doing it and realizing how convenient this feature truly is.
  • ✓ Resistance control is buttery smooth with the digital knob. The micro-adjustments mean you can hit exact resistance levels, and the instant feedback on screen lets you match metrics with precision that manual bikes simply can't match.
  • ✓ The bike's build quality and stability are undeniable. After months of intense threshold efforts and jump intervals, zero creaking, zero wobble—this machine is engineered to handle aggressive workouts without feeling flimsy or questionable.
Cons
  • ✗ The subscription model creates ongoing costs beyond the initial purchase. The Peloton Plus membership is required to access most classes, and that's roughly $44 monthly or $456 yearly on top of an already significant upfront investment. The bike becomes limited utility without that subscription active.
  • ✗ Space requirements are substantial—the footprint is roughly 4 feet by 2 feet, and you'll want clearance around it for safety and comfort. This isn't a closet-friendly option, which eliminates it for anyone living in smaller apartments or homes without dedicated gym space.

Our Verdict

The Peloton Bike+ justifies its price if—and this is conditional—you're someone who thrives on structured, instructor-led workouts and will actually use it consistently. The 4.3-star rating from 500+ real users reflects genuine satisfaction for the right buyer. The touchscreen, build quality, and class library are genuinely excellent. However, the subscription requirement and space demands mean this isn't the right bike for everyone. If you're an independent rider who wants just the hardware, or someone severely space-limited, consider alternatives. But if you're motivated by live and on-demand classes, enjoy competition (through leaderboards), and have the room and budget for ongoing costs, this machine delivers on its promises. Test it somewhere first if possible—the touchscreen experience and seat comfort are deeply personal, and that initial investment deserves hands-on validation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you need the Peloton subscription to use the Bike+?

Technically, no—the bike functions as a standard stationary bike without the subscription. However, most features you're actually paying for, including the class library, instructor-led workouts, and leaderboard competition, require Peloton Plus membership. The subscription is essentially mandatory if you want the full connected experience. You can view metrics and manually cycle, but that defeats the purpose of choosing this premium bike over a $500 alternative.

How does the Peloton Bike+ compare to the original Bike?

The main differences are the rotating 24-inch screen (versus the fixed 21.5-inch screen on the original) and slight hardware refinements. If screen size and rotating capability matter to you—particularly for non-cycling content—it's worth the upgrade. Otherwise, the original Bike offers nearly identical ride mechanics at lower cost. I'd only recommend the Bike+ if you plan to use the screen flexibility or absolutely need the largest display.

Is the Peloton Bike+ suitable for beginners?

Yes, but with context. The instructors provide scaling modifications for beginner riders, and the touchscreen makes it easy to control intensity. However, beginners should know that Peloton's culture is performance-focused. You'll see your metrics compared to others, which is motivating for some and discouraging for others. The bike itself has a learning curve—clipping in pedals, adjusting resistance correctly, and understanding proper form all take time. Start with the beginner series and don't judge yourself against experienced riders logging 100+ rides per year.
